Structure of a Cell

What Exactly is a Cell?

A cell is the smallest unit of life and contains parts called “organelles,” which are essentially structures within the cell that have specialized functions.1

There are two main types of cells: eukaryotic and prokaryotic. Eukaryotic cells differ from prokaryotic cells as they contain a nucleus around the genetic material, have membrane-bound organelles, and have linearly structured DNA (prokaryotic cells have circularly structured DNA).

  • Eukaryotic cells make up multicellular (and some unicellular) organisms, such as animals, plants, fungi, algae, and protozoans.
  • Prokaryotic cells make up unicellular organisms: bacteria and archaea.2

Structure of Animal and Plant Cells

(Animal cells and plant cells are Eukaryotic)

Mitochondria: “The powerhouse of the cell.” Creates energy for the cell from glucose and other nutrients via cellular respiration. 3

Cytoplasm: A gelatinous liquid that helps hold the organelles in place and retains the circular shape of the cell.4

Lysosome: Organelle that recycles worn-out cell parts and digests viruses/bacteria. Can also perform apoptosis.5

Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum: Creates lipids as well as stores them. Differs from the rough endoplasmic reticulum as it does not have ribosomes (Rogers, "smooth endoplasmic reticulum").6

Ribosomes: Organelle that is made up of a combination of proteins and rRNA (ribosomal RNA). Creates proteins for the cell through a process called “translation."7

Centriole: Complex of microtubules that help separate the chromosomes during cell division. They are also responsible for the synthesis of microtubules.8

Golgi body: Otherwise known as the Golgi apparatus, it is composed of sacs called cisternae and helps receive and transport proteins using vesicles.9

Nucleus: A spherical structure that contains the cell’s chromosomes and another organelle called the nucleolus.

  • The nucleolus is responsible for assembling ribosomes.10

Plasma Membrane: The membrane that creates the outside of the cell and separates the cell’s insides from the outside environment.11

Plant cells are similar to animal cells, except plant cells are rectangular and have a few different organelles, such as:

  • Chloroplasts: Are responsible for photosynthesis.12
  • Cell wall: A wall-like structure outside of the plasma membrane. Helps keep structural integrity and protects the cell. It also gives plant cells their rectangular shape.13

Structure of a Prokaryotic Cell

Unlike eukaryotic cells, prokaryotic cells have no nucleus and no membrane-bound organelles. However, they have some similar cell structures, such as ribosomes, cell membranes, and cytoplasm. Some prokaryotes can even have cell walls.14

Prokaryotic cells can also have tail-like structures such as flagellum (which helps the cells move) and Pilus (used to attach to other bacteria). Some types of bacteria can also have another layer called the capsule, which helps protect the cell as well as attain its moisture.15
