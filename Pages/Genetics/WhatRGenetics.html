What Is Genetics?

Genetics is the study of how traits (or genes) get passed down from generation to generation. These genes are located within our cells in thread-like structures called “Deoxyribonucleic acid” or “DNA”1.

The study originated from a scientist known as Gregor Mendel, who made many contributions to the field of Biology via his studies on hereditary. His breeding of peas helped him come up with multiple theories on how genes are passed down and was the creator of the words “dominant” and “recessive” to describe the phenotypical (physical) traits of an organism2.

The complete genetic information of a human is called a genome. Genomes are what allow us to grow and to tell the body how it should behave, and what traits should or should not be expressed. The human genome consists of 23 pairs of chromosomes (46 in total)3.
