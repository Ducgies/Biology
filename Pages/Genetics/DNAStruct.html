Structure Of DNA

What is DNA Made Out of?

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic acid) is made up of connected molecules called “nucleotides” and is arranged in a structure called a “double-helix,” which looks like a twisted ladder1.

What is a Nucleotide?

Nucleotides are molecules that makeup DNA. They are composed of three parts: a sugar called “deoxyribose,” a base, and a phosphate. Nucleotides can come with one of four different types of nitrogenous bases: adenine, thymine, cytosine, and guanine1.

Adenosine and Guanine are considered “purines” in which they have two rings. Cytosine and Guanine only have one ring, so they are called “pyrimidines.” Purines pair up with pyrimidines, which means that adenine pairs with thymine (or uracil for RNA), and guanine pairs with cytosine2.

How Does DNA Affect Our Phenotype?

The nitrogenous bases serve as parts of a code. When they are arranged in a specific order, they code for a certain protein, thus causing us to express our physical traits3.

When using the DNA’s code to create a specific protein, two processes need to occur: transcription and translation. Transcription is the phenomenon in which a DNA sequence needs to be copied as mRNA (messenger RNA) so that it can be sent out of the nucleus and into a ribosome. To do this, an enzyme called “RNA Polymerase” is used, and it “unzips” the DNA strand so that it can create a copy of the sequence. There is a “template strand” that the RNA Polymerase runs down, and it matches the nitrogenous bases of the template stand (for example, RNA polymerase connects an adenosine base with a thymine base). This way, the RNA Polymerase can create a replica of the strand it is not copying, otherwise known as a “non-template strand.” Once the enzyme is done creating the mRNA, the mRNA goes outside of the nucleus and into the ribosome. Once in the ribosome, a process called “translation” occurs, and a specific chain of amino acids (called polypeptides) is produced depending on the sequence of the mRNA. The amino acids then fold onto each other and create a protein4.
