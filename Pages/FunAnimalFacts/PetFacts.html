Pet Facts

Some cool facts about our pets!

Dogs and Cats are Colorblind

This isn’t to say that dogs and cats only see in black in white. They can still see in color. However, they don’t see as many colors as we humans do. Humans have three cone photoreceptors, which allow us to see red, blue, and green. Dogs and cats, however, only have two cones for blue and green1.

Dogs tend to see color in the yellow part of the spectrum, so red, yellow, green, and orange are harder for dogs to distinguish between. It is said that cats tend to see blues and grays, but some researchers believe that they also only see the yellow part of the spectrum, just like dogs2.

There’s a reason why cats and dogs don’t have as many cones as humans. According to Dr. Patty Khuly on the Embrace Pet Insurance website,

“Pets don’t need to see as many colors. After all, they’re hunters who are primarily carnivorous. They don’t need to worry so much about mistaking one kind of edible plant for another, for example. As predators, they’re much more concerned about being able to see their prey move on a landscape or in a forest. Contrast is everything”

Dogs Have an Incredible Sense of Smell

A dog’s nose has around 300 million olfactory receptors, while humans only have six million. In fact, if you were to put one teaspoon of sugar in two Olympic-sized pools, a dog would be able to sniff it out3.

Because of their incredible sense of smell, they are often used to smell for drugs, bombs, and dead bodies. Dogs are also used experimentally to sniff out diseases in humans, such as cancer4.

Cats Spend 70% of Their Lives Sleeping

Cats are known to be big nappers, but did you know that the average cat spends around 70% of their life sleeping5? On average, cats sleep for 15 hours a day. Kittens and older cats can even sleep for 20 hours a day6!

However, unlike how humans sleep only once during the nighttime, cats sleep multiple times throughout the day, but usually very lightly. In fact, their deep sleep is only around five minutes, as their naps can take about 15-20 minutes5.

Goldfish Actually Have Good Memories

There’s a common myth that goldfish only have three-second memories, but that isn’t true! In fact, goldfish can have memories that can last for weeks, months, or years. They can also remember the faces of their owners7.

There are actually multiple studies that showed how good goldfish memories are. According to Adam Jones8 from The Goldfish Tank website:

“At least two separate studies into goldfish memory have proven that they remember things for more than just three seconds. In the first study, scientists added a lever to a goldfish tank, which dispensed food when pressed. Once goldfish learned to press the lever, they would regularly return to it to get more food. Even more amazingly, the goldfish actually learned to press the lever at the right time of day! Researchers adjusted the lever so that it would only dispense food within a one-hour window… and the fish learned to come back each day around that time!”

(“Carassius auratus auratus (goldfish) 1” by James St. John. Licensed under CC BY 2.0/resized from original)

Hamsters are Nocturnal

Unlike humans, which are diurnal (awake during the day), and cats/dogs, which are crepuscular (awake during dawn)9 hamsters are nocturnal, which means they are awake at night. 41% of their time is also spent on sleeping. Some hamsters can be diurnal, but it is rare. Furthermore, wild hamsters tend to be crepuscular, just like cats10.

The reason why hamsters are nocturnal is to prevent themselves from being seen during the night by predators. This also allows them to look for food as there are fewer dangers (Hamsteropedia). The nose-whiskers of hamsters allow them to maneuver during the night, as well as their sense of smell10.
