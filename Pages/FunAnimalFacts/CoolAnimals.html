Cool Animals

Boops Boops

Did you know there is a type of fish called “Boop boops”? It’s true! The fish’s scientific name is Boops boops and is native to the eastern Atlantic Ocean, Mediterranean, and black seas. The fish is also referred to as a Bogue1.

("Boops Boops Israel" by Peter van der Sluijs is licensed under CC BY-SA 3.0/resized from original)

Mimic Octopus

There is an octopus located on the coast of Sulawesi Indonesia that can look like and impersonate other venomous creatures such as Sole fish, Lionfish, and Sea snakes (hence the name “mimic” octopus). Its scientific name is Thaumoctopus mimicus2.

(“Mimic Octopus" by Elias Levy is licensed under CC BY 2.0/resized from original)

Blue Dragon

The Blue Dragon can be found in the Indian Ocean. They are a species of snail and their scientific name is Glaucus atlanticus. Blue Dragons are very small animals, with the average being 3 centimeters. However, even though they may look cute, they are incredibly venomous creatures, and should not be touched3.

(“Glaucus atlanticus Long reef” by Peter Woodard (Poyt448). Licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0/resized from original)

Axolotl

The Axolotl is a type of salamander that is only found in Lake Xochimilco, Mexico City. Their scientific name is Ambystoma mexicanum. Usually, salamanders have gills when they are young, but then lose them when they grow into adults. Axolotls, however, never lose their gills. These cute animals are sadly a critically endangered species4.

(Image from LaDameBucolique via Pixabay)

Mantis Shrimp

Mantis shrimp are a type of shrimp with over 450 species5. They can have either “spears” or “smashers” (depending on the shrimp) in order to capture and kill their prey. The “spear” is like claws that can impale prey, while the “smasher” is a club that hits the prey incredibly hard, thus smashing them6. Mantis shrimp also have some of the best vision, as their eyes have 16 kinds of photoreceptors7.

(“Peacock Mantis Shrimp” by Christian Gloor. Licensed under CC BY 2.0/resized from original)
