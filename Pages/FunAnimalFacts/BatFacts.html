Bat Facts

(this webpage is dedicated to my brother, who likes bats☺)

Bats are the Only Mammals Capable of Flight

The only mammals that are able to fly are bats. Flying squirrels and flying lemurs don’t actually fly, they instead glide. But bats on the other hand are true fliers1. They use their wings that are made out of a thin membrane, with a thumb pointing out of it so that they can hang on to trees2.

Many bats, however, cannot actually take off from the ground. They have to jump off ledges or cliffs to get themselves going. However, some species, such as the horseshoe and vampire bats, can2.

(“Short-tailed Fruit Bat” taken by Andy Morffew. Licensed under CC BY 2.0/resized from original)

Some Bats can Sleep up to 20 Hours a Day

Just like how cats are incredibly sleepy creatures, bats are also very sleepy! They can sleep for 20 hours a day3. They sleep upside down because they usually can’t take off from the ground when they need to fly, and can’t have a running start because of their tiny legs. Instead, bats let go of what they were hanging from4. Bats also don’t always sleep in caves, they can sleep in many other places too, such as on trees or roofs3.

(“Sleeping Bat” taken by Petr Kratochvil. Licensed under CC0 1.0)

Bats are not Actually Blind

The idea of bats being blind is actually false! Bats have fantastic sight and can even have better eyesight than humans. They also have echolocation, not because they are blind, but because it helps maneuver them in the dark5. They use their eyes to help them find food during the day, and their ears (echolocation) to help find food during the night6.

(“Batnightflyingjf” taken by Ramon FVelasquez. Licensed under CC BY-SA 3.0/resized from original)

Bats Have Great Immune Systems

As the caution goes, people need to be careful of getting bit by a bat, as they can get rabies from them. But for some odd reason, bats rarely die from it7. Why is that?

Bats have incredible immune systems. They can host many deadly viruses such as rabies, Ebola, Nipah, and SARS8. The reason why they can sustain said viruses is because of their “larger-than-expected families of genes related to the mammalian immune system” according to Barbara Moran from the website Futurity.

(“Myotis bats” by Bureau of Land Management California. Licensed under CC BY 2.0/resized from original)

The Brazillian Free-Tailed Bats are Incredibly Fast

The Brazillian free-tailed bat can reach speeds up to 99.42 miles per hour! This makes the species the fastest flying animal at level flight in the world9. Bats are usually considered slow fliers due to the shape of their wings, but this species of bat is the exception10!

(“Mexican Free-tailed Bat” taken by Ron Groves, EOL Learning and Education Group. Licensed under CC BY-NC-SA 2.0/resized from original)
