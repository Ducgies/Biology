What is Evolution?

Evolution is the change of species throughout time. It is driven by a process called ‘natural selection” in which the “fittest” organisms thrive and reproduce, while the ones who are less fit die, thus causing a change in the species throughout time. The founding fathers of natural selection are Charles Darwin and Alfred Russel Wallace. Charles Darwin helped create the theory of evolution through his studies on natural selection, as well as his published book “On the Origin of Species." 1
